1. Combine descriptions of repeated actions performed consecutively into a single statement that reflects how a user might naturally group these steps.
2. Ensure that all possible details provided to a component in brackets must be included in the generated prompt. These details are placed directly after the respective operation they belong to, and only refer to that specific operation. How you incorporate these details is up to you—feel free to experiment with different approaches, just as a real user would, to ensure all relevant information is captured.

# Steps

1. Apply all important guidelines.
2. Use varied introductions that resemble how real users might ask a conversational AI to design a microfluidic chip.
3. Display the several steps involved in the microfluidic processes as a list of bullet points, ensuring that each step is written on a separate line.
4. Use typical names of fluids involved in microfluidics, as a real user might employ these in their descriptions.
5. Use synonyms for microfluidic components that may be mentioned in the prompt and replace repeated words and formulations with varied expressions that still keep the meaning clear and sound more conversational.
6. Convert the prompt into different perspectives.
